Trust us to help you through your ordeal so you can focus on healing and moving forward after your truck accident. Moving Forward after a Truck Accident.

Insurance adjusters may show up at the scene of an accident and attempt to catch truck accident victims off guard with leading and incriminating questions. Here are some ways victims can protect themselves after being involved in a truck accident. Don't sign anything presented to you by an insurance adjuster with speaking to the truck accident lawyers at the Pipkin Law Firm. You could be damaging your claim and signing away any future rights to compensation. See your doctor immediately. Any delay may harm your case.

Keep records of all the treatment you receive, medications, and physician recommendations. Remember that you have the right to choose your physician-don't let the adjuster choose one for you.

Call your insurance company immediately to avoid a delay that could harm your case.

Keep records after the accident. Write down how you felt, including details like how difficult and painful it was just to pull on a shirt in the morning or lift your arms to brush your teeth. It may be difficult to remember how painful recovering was six or seven months later, and a record like this will help guard against claims by an insurance adjuster that you are exaggerating your injuries. Also keep bills for services you needed after the accident, such as lawn mowing or maid services.

Take photos at the scene of the accident, if you are able, and of your injuries after the accident and as you heal. Photographic evidence is extremely difficult to dispute and can strengthen your truck accident lawsuit.

Personal Injury Statute of Limitations Texas

After a car accident injury, it is completely normal to take care of your health first. For many people, hiring a lawyer to start the legal process sounds stressful. But keep in mind, you don’t have an unlimited amount of time to file. Every state’s statute of limitations surrounding car accident cases is different.

So, what does “statute of limitations” mean? It means that there is a limit on the amount of time you have to file a lawsuit. In the cases of personal injury and claims surrounding property damage, you have two years to file a petition with the courts in Texas (there are some exceptions to this). Two years is not an unlimited amount of time so don’t procrastinate.

The sooner you take action, the better. Keep in mind that contacting a lawyer should NOT add stress. An attorney’s goal is to relieve the stress of your legal claim and handle the process entirely ourselves.
